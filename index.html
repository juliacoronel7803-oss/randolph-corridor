South LA Infrastructure Capstone Project

The Randolph Line Green Equity Corridor

Transforming 4 miles of abandoned railway into a climate-resilient, community-owned transportation network.

Addressing the "Redzone" Reality

The Randolph corridor is currently a site of systemic environmental neglect, creating a health and safety crisis for South LA residents.

Urban Heat Island

Lack of tree canopy means temperatures are 5-10°F hotter, increasing heat-related illnesses.

Air Quality Crisis

Proximity to industrial areas and heavy traffic leads to high rates of asthma and respiratory disease.

Safety & Barrier

The abandoned line is a physical barrier and magnet for illegal dumping, impacting community safety.

Our Triple Bottom Line: Health, Wealth, and Justice

The Randolph Corridor is designed for measurable impact in the three areas where South LA needs it most.

Improved Public Health & Climate Resilience

  • Tree Canopy & Cool Pavement: Reduces ambient temperatures by up to 10°F, directly fighting the Urban Heat Island effect.
  • Bioswales: Filters polluted stormwater runoff, contributing to cleaner local waterways.
  • Active Mobility: Class IV bike lanes and pedestrian paths encourage cycling and walking, improving cardiovascular health.

Local Job Creation & Economic ROI

  • High ROI: Every $1.00 invested in this corridor returns approximately $1.72 in local economic activity.
  • Jobs: Projected to create 15 local jobs per $1 million invested in construction and long-term maintenance.
  • Business Support: Increased foot traffic and visibility for neighborhood businesses along the route.

Guaranteeing Community Equity

  • Anti-Displacement Plan: Includes mandated rent stabilization and a local housing trust fund to protect residents from gentrification.
  • Local Hiring: 40% of all project jobs are reserved for residents within the South LA ZIP codes.
  • Community Control: Future governance will include a resident-majority oversight board for long-term stewardship.
